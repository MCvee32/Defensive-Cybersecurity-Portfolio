# Part 1 - A15: Discover 5 Recent Security Incidents

### Incident 1: Ticketmaster Data Breach (2024)
In 2024, Ticketmaster reported a large data breach potentially affecting hundreds of millions of customers around the world. The data breach is linked to a hacking group, called ShinyHunters, gaining unauthorised access to a thrid-party cloud database. The incident involved the theft of personal information including names, email addresses, and payment details.

### Incident 2: Microsoft Email Breach (2024)
In 2024, Microsoft reported that a Russian-linked hacking group had gained access to internal company emails. Attackers compromised a legacy account and accessed communications from senior executives and staff via a password spray attack.

**Evidence**
https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/msrc/blog/2024/01/microsoft-actions-following-attack-by-nation-state-actor-midnight-blizzard

### Incident 3: London NHS Hospitals (Synnovis) Ransomware Attack (2024)
In 2024, a ransomware attack on Synnovis, a pathology service provider, severly disrupted London hospitals with 1,134 elective procedures and 2,194 outpatient appointments being postponed. The cybercriminal group claimed to have stolen 400GB of data.

### Incident 4: QANTAS Data Breach (2025)
In 2025, QANTAS confirmed a data theft incident that impacted up to 6 million customers. This resulted from an attack on an offshore IT call centre, enabling cybercriminals to gain unauthorised access to a third-party system containing basic customer information and loyalty program details. This highlighted security risks associated with third-party systems and attacks on vulnerable entry points to less secure systems.

**Evidence**
https://www.theguardian.com/business/2025/jul/06/qantas-attack-reveals-one-phone-call-is-all-it-takes-to-crack-cybersecuritys-weakest-link-humans
