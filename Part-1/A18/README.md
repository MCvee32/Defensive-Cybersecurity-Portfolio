# Part 1 - A18: Discover 2 Hallucination Cases When Using a Generative AI System

### Case 1: Mata v. Avianca, Inc. Fake Legal Citations
In this case a lawyer used ChatGPT to assist with legal research. The AI generated case law citations that appeared legitimate but were complected fabricated. These fake cases were then submitted in a court filing.
This is a hallucination case as the AI produced confident but false information, iventing legal cases that didn't exist.

### Case 2: False Medical Advice
GenAI systems have been reported to provide incorrect or misleading medical advice, such as inappropriate treatments or misdiagnosing users. For example, in August 2025, a man asked ChatGPT for a substitute for table salt in his diet. The AI suggested sodium bromide without warning that it is toxic to humans. After consuming it over time, the man developed bromism and was hospitalised with psychiatric symptoms. Sodium bromide is a suitable substitution for salt in the context of disinfecting pools, not in diets. This case illustrates a clear hallucination of GenAI in medical advice.
