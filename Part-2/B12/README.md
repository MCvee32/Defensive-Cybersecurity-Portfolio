# Part 2 - B12: Discover two bias cases when using a generative AI system

### Case 1: Profession Gender Bias
When prompted for images of a "CEO" or "tradesman", generators will more often than not produce images of white men. Conversely, prompts for "nurse" or "housekeeper" generate images of woman.

### Case 2: Racial Bias in Healthcare Diagnosis
AI models often produce lower-quality diagnoses, less accurate risk assessments, and inequitable treatment recommendations for patients of color. This occurs when algorithms are trained on skewed historical or structural data.

**Evidence**
- Study from Cedars-Sinai. Where they found AI generative tools exhibiting bias when treating African American patients with psychiatric disorders. In the study, when a patient's race was stated or implied the AI system often produced worse or different treatment recommendations.
